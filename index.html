Biography of the Maestro Fresh Wes

Wesley Williams, also known as Maestro Fresh Wes or simply Maestro, was born in 1968 in Toronto, ON. He is known as a Canadian rapper, author, and actor and is often called the godfather of hip-hop. When he was 8 years old, he started taking piano lessons. His brother played the drums, and his father supported him while he was learning new instruments. When he was 11 years old, he started writing poetry and decided to become a rapper when he grew up. As he got older, he developed a strong interest in hip-hop culture, learning how to breakdance, practicing spitting rhymes, and listening religiously to CKLN Radio's hip-hop show, which was known as The Fantastic Voyage in the radio.

Investigating symbols, metaphors, and allusions and uses of irony and imagery in his songs?

Symbols

  • "I Can't Breathe" by Maestro Fresh Wes represents George Floyd's death at the hands of a racist officer. He wrote this song to address the struggle for justice for Black people and others, and he also wanted to advocate for equal rights for all people living in the world.
  • This song symbolizes the lack of justice and the increase of racism in North America.








Metaphors

  • "These shackles on my feet": They won't let me be. Shackles represent slavery and a lack of freedom.
  • "Your deception is like a gun in my face: it identifies lies and violent threats."








Allusions

  • This references slavery, civil rights, and the lack of justice in America.

History of the war events in the song

What happened?

The event happened on May 25, 2020. A 46-year-old man named George Floyd went to a convenience store, and an employee called 911, telling the police that Mr. Floyd had bought cigarettes with a fake $20 bill. Seventeen minutes after the police officers arrived at the scene, they made Mr. Floyd unconscious. The officers knelt on his neck, and poor Floyd repeatedly told the officers, "I can't breathe," three times. However, the officers did not take him seriously because of his skin color. After he said, "I can't breathe" three times, he passed away because of these unscrupulous cops.



Why did the artist chose this song



The artist chose this song because he wanted to remind American citizens of the death of George Floyd and also wanted to tell the government that we should not be racist toward anyone, as we are all human. All humans need to respect each other; if we do not, we will be like animals, killing our own kind. He wanted to convey this message through his song, emphasizing that we are humans, we need to respect each other, and we should not judge others by their skin, race, or culture. We should all be treated equally.
