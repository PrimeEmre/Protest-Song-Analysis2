Biograhy of the Maestro Fresh Wes

Wesley Williams, also known as Maestro Fresh Wes or simply Maestro, was born in 1968 in Toronto, ON. He is known as a Canadian rapper, author, and actor and is often called the godfather of hip-hop. When he was 8 years old, he started taking piano lessons. His brother played the drums, and his father supported him while he was learning new instruments. When he was 11 years old, he started writing poetry and decided to become a rapper when he grew up. As he got older, he developed a strong interest in hip-hop culture, learning how to breakdance, practicing spitting rhymes, and listening religiously to CKLN Radio's hip-hop show, which was known as The Fantastic Voyage in the raido.

Symbols

  • "I Can't Breathe" by Maestro Fresh Wes represents George Floyd's death at the hands of a racist officer. He wrote this song to address the struggle for justice for Black people and others, and he also wanted to advocate for equal rights for all people living in the world.
  • This song symbolizes the lack of justice and the increase of racism in North America.
